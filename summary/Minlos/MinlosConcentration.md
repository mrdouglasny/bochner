## Overview

This file proves the **nuclear cylindrical concentration bound** (Gel'fand-Vilenkin Vol. 4, Ch. IV, Section 3.3): a cylindrical probability measure $\nu$ on $E^{\mathbb{N}}$ (evaluation functionals) with continuous characteristic functional $\Phi$ satisfies a dimension-free concentration bound for $\mathbb{Q}$-linear combinations of vectors. The proof combines CF continuity giving quadratic bounds in seminorms, Gaussian averaging on orthonormal bases with Hilbert-Schmidt bounds, Cauchy-Schwarz and Parseval on the "good set" (bounded evaluation norm), and Chebyshev plus continuity from below to extend to the full space. Two theorems are proved: `nuclear_cylindrical_concentration` (main) and `minlos_concentration` (wrapper). Both are fully proved with zero sorries.

### Main theorem

### [`nuclear_cylindrical_concentration`](../../Minlos/MinlosConcentration.lean#L1669) -- Theorem

**Statement**: Given a separable nonempty locally convex space $E$ with topology generated by Hilbertian seminorms $\{p_n\}$ with consecutive Hilbert-Schmidt embeddings, and a cylindrical probability measure $\nu$ with continuous CF $\Phi$ ($\Phi(0) = 1$, joint CF property), for any dense sequence $d : \mathbb{N} \to E$ and any $\varepsilon > 0$, there exist $m, C \in \mathbb{N}$ such that
$$\nu\Bigl\{\omega : \exists\, c \in \mathbb{N} \to_0 \mathbb{Q},\;\; \lvert \omega(c \cdot d) \rvert > C \cdot p_m(c \cdot d)\Bigr\} < \varepsilon.$$

**Proof uses**: [`cf_norm_le_one`](../../Minlos/MinlosConcentration.lean#L86), [`combined_quadratic_bound`](../../Minlos/MinlosConcentration.lean#L189), [`gram_schmidt_seminorm`](../../Minlos/MinlosConcentration.lean#L685), [`tail_bound_uniform`](../../Minlos/MinlosConcentration.lean#L1229), [`kernel_concentration_bound`](../../Minlos/MinlosConcentration.lean#L1010), [`badSetN_bound_with_kernel`](../../Minlos/MinlosConcentration.lean#L1605), [`concentrationBadSet_measure_le`](../../Minlos/MinlosConcentration.lean#L455), [`concentrationBadSetN_mono`](../../Minlos/MinlosConcentration.lean#L428)
