## Overview

This file proves that the Fourier transform of a continuous, integrable, positive-definite function on a finite-dimensional real inner product space has nonnegative real part. The proof proceeds by Fejer averaging: the double integral $\frac{1}{\mu(B_R)} \iint_{B_R \times B_R} \psi(x-y)\,dx\,dy$ has $\Re \geq 0$ (Step A via Riemann-sum approximation), converges to $\int \psi$ by Fubini and dominated convergence (Step B), and the limit inequality follows by `ge_of_tendsto'` (Step C).
