### Phase 4: Tightness and Weak Limit

The Fourier transform of a Gaussian-regularized PD function is integrable. Proof: $\widehat{\varphi_\varepsilon} \geq 0$ (from PD), and for each $t > 0$ the Parseval/Fubini identity gives $\int \widehat{\varphi_\varepsilon} \cdot g_t = \int \varphi_\varepsilon \cdot \widehat{g_t}$, bounded by $\|\varphi_\varepsilon\|_\infty \cdot \|\widehat{g_t}\|_1 = |\varphi(0)|$. Monotone convergence (as $g_t \nearrow 1$) gives $\int_{\text{essup}} \|\widehat{\varphi_\varepsilon}\| \leq |\varphi(0)| < \infty$ (Folland §4.2).

Proof uses: [pd_l1_fourier_nonneg](#pd_l1_fourier_nonneg), Parseval identity ([parseval_l1](#parseval_l1)), monotone convergence via Fatou's lemma.

The family of measures $\{\mu_\varepsilon\}$ constructed from Gaussian regularization is tight. For each $\varepsilon > 0$, $\mu_\varepsilon$ is the probability measure with $\text{charFun}(\mu_\varepsilon) = \varphi_\varepsilon$. Tightness follows from: for any probability measure $\mu$, $\mu(\{|\langle a, x \rangle| > r\}) \leq C \cdot r \cdot \int_{-2/r}^{2/r} (1 - \text{Re}(\text{charFun}(\mu)(t \cdot a))) \, dt$ (Mathlib: `measureReal_abs_inner_gt_le_integral_charFun`). ### Phase 5: Main Theorem

[**bochner_theorem**](../../Bochner/Bochner.lean#L1190) — `theorem (φ : V → ℂ) (hcont : Continuous φ) (hpd : IsPositiveDefinite φ) (hnorm : φ 0 = 1)`

**Bochner's Theorem.** A continuous positive-definite function $\varphi : V \to \mathbb{C}$ with $\varphi(0) = 1$ on a finite-dimensional real inner product space is the characteristic function of a unique probability measure on $V$. The finite-dimensional version is stated here; the infinite-dimensional generalization (Bochner-Minlos) additionally requires nuclearity of the domain space.

**Existence** (Phases 2–4): Gaussian regularization $\varphi_\varepsilon$ is PD (gaussianRegularize_pd) and integrable (gaussianRegularize_integrable). Each $\varphi_\varepsilon$ with $\varphi_\varepsilon(0) = 1$ yields a probability measure $\mu_\varepsilon$ via Fourier inversion (measure_of_pd_l1), with $\text{charFun}(\mu_\varepsilon) = \varphi_\varepsilon$. The family $\{\mu_\varepsilon\}$ is tight (gaussianRegularize_measures_tight), so Prokhorov's theorem extracts a weakly convergent subsequence $\mu_{\varepsilon_k} \rightharpoonup \mu$. Since $\text{charFun}(\mu_{\varepsilon_k}) \to \varphi$ pointwise (gaussianRegularize_tendsto), weak convergence implies $\text{charFun}(\mu) = \varphi$.

**Uniqueness**: From Mathlib's `Measure.ext_of_charFun`: the characteristic function determines the measure uniquely.

Proof uses: [measure_of_pd_l1](#measure_of_pd_l1), [gaussianRegularize_pd](#gaussianregularize_pd), [gaussianRegularize_integrable](#gaussianregularize_integrable), [gaussianRegularize_ft_integrable](#gaussianregularize_ft_integrable), [gaussianRegularize_measures_tight](#gaussianregularize_measures_tight), [gaussianRegularize_tendsto](#gaussianregularize_tendsto), Prokhorov compactness ([isCompact_closure_of_isTightMeasureSet](#iscompact_closure_of_istightmeasureset)), weak convergence (ProbabilityMeasure.tendsto_iff_forall_integral_rclike_tendsto), Mathlib's Measure.ext_of_charFun.
